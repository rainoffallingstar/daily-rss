Prehospital treatment of severely burned patients: a retrospective analysis of patients admitted to the Berlin burn centre

CONCLUSION: We showed that severely burned patients frequently received excessive fluid administration prehospitally and that this was not associated with more hemodynamic stability or outcome. In our cohort, patients were frequently intubated prehospitally, which was associated with increased mortality rates.

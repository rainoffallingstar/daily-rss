We The People: Legal Representation

And in fact, the right to an attorney is written into the Constitution’s sixth amendment. But for most of U.S. history, it was more of a nice-to-have — something you got if you could, but that many people went without. Today, though, public defenders represent up to 80% of people charged with crimes. So what changed?

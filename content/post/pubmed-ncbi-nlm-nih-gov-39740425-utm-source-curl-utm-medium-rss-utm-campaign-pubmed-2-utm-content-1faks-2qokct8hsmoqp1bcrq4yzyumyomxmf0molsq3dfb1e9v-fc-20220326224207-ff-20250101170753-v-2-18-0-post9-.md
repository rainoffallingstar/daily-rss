CONCLUSIONS: Our findings indicate strong alignment between healthcare professionals' and people with lung cancer evaluations of observable treatment-related symptoms, but less consistency for subjective symptoms such as dyspnoea. Factors such as prior chemotherapy, COPD, and cancer stage should be considered when interpreting symptom assessments. Furthermore, our study underscores the importance of integrating quality-of-life considerations, particularly fatigue, into symptom evaluations ...

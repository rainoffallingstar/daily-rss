CONCLUSIONS: We found choroidal caverns in almost a quarter of patients with and without T2DM. CCs were mainly located in the Sattler's layer. CVI was independently associated with the presence of CCs. These findings suggest that the presence of CCs may be a sign of a higher metabolic activity within the choroidal microenvironment, irrespective of T2DM ...
